Understanding Pounds Lost

How to Calculate Pounds Lost

Calculating how many pounds you've lost is simple subtraction. You take your starting weight and subtract your current weight to find the total pounds lost.

Formula: Pounds Lost = Starting Weight - Current Weight

For example, if you started at 200 pounds and now weigh 180 pounds:

  • Pounds lost = 200 - 180 = 20 pounds

This means you've successfully lost 20 pounds through your diet, exercise, and lifestyle changes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate how many pounds I've lost?
Use our calculator above or simply subtract your current weight from your starting weight. For example: Starting 200 lbs - Current 180 lbs = 20 pounds lost.
Why track pounds lost instead of just percentage?
While percentage provides important context, tracking pounds lost gives you tangible numbers that are easy to understand and relate to daily life. Pounds directly correlate with clothing sizes and provide clear, measurable goals.
How many pounds should I aim to lose per week?
A safe and sustainable rate of weight loss is 1-2 pounds per week. Losing weight gradually increases the likelihood of keeping it off long-term. Rapid weight loss can lead to muscle loss and nutritional deficiencies.

Benefits of Tracking in Kilograms

Precise Measurement

Kilograms provide a more precise measurement than pounds, with clear decimal increments that make tracking small changes easier.

International Standard

The metric system is used by most countries worldwide, making it easier to compare and share your progress with others globally.

Easy Calculations

Calculating percentages and other metrics is simpler with the decimal-based metric system compared to imperial measurements.

Health Professional Standard

Most healthcare providers and nutritionists use kilograms, making it easier to discuss your progress with medical professionals.

Tracking Tip: Weigh yourself at the same time each day (preferably in the morning after using the bathroom) for the most consistent measurements. Focus on long-term trends rather than daily fluctuations.

Understanding Your Weight Loss in Kilograms

How to Calculate Kilograms Lost

Calculating how many kilograms you've lost is simple: subtract your current weight from your starting weight. This gives you the total weight loss in kilograms.

Formula: Kilograms Lost = Starting Weight (kg) - Current Weight (kg)
Example: If you started at 95 kg and now weigh 85 kg, you've lost 10 kg (95 - 85 = 10).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate how many kilograms I've lost?
Simply subtract your current weight from your starting weight. For example, if you started at 90 kg and now weigh 80 kg, you've lost 10 kg. Our calculator does this automatically and also calculates your weight loss percentage.
How is weight loss percentage calculated?
Weight loss percentage is calculated by dividing the kilograms lost by your starting weight, then multiplying by 100. For example, if you lost 10 kg from a starting weight of 90 kg, your weight loss percentage is (10/90)*100 = 11.1%.
Why track weight loss in kilograms?
Tracking in kilograms provides a precise measurement of your progress. The metric system is used by most of the world and provides clear, decimal-based measurements that are easy to track over time. Kilograms are also the standard measurement in healthcare settings.
How often should I weigh myself?
For most people, weighing once a week is sufficient to track progress without becoming obsessed with daily fluctuations. Always weigh yourself at the same time of day, preferably in the morning after using the bathroom and before eating or drinking.
